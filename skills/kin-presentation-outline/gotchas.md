# Gotchas

Accumulated failure patterns. Add a line each time the skill produces a bad outcome.

## Scoring
- **Auto-audit self-grading inflation.** When you generate an outline and then audit it, you will overrate your own output because you know the intent behind each section. Score conservatively. If you cannot quote a specific phrase as evidence, score 2/5 maximum. Expect your auto-audit scores to be 0.5-1.0 points higher than an independent audit would produce.
- **Passion/Inspiration is not scoreable from text.** Don't try to infer enthusiasm from word choice — it's unfalsifiable. This is why it's an unscored delivery reminder, not a rubric dimension.
- **Environment/Setup is rarely in outlines.** Most outlines don't mention room lighting or time slots. This is why it's a logistics checklist, not a scored dimension.

## Mode Detection
- **"Improve" without content routes to Build, not Audit.** "Help me improve my presentation" (no outline provided) means they want to create or restructure, not audit an existing doc.

## Framework Application
- **Don't force cycling on short talks.** A 5-minute lightning talk with 3 cycling passes is incoherent. Zero cycles is correct for lightning talks — one clear through-line.
- **"No thank you" is culturally contested.** Winston says never end with "thank you," but many professional contexts expect it. Present both sides. Don't dogmatically enforce Winston's rule if the user's context calls for it.
- **Board orthodoxy doesn't transfer to modern venues.** Most conference rooms and Zoom calls don't have chalkboards. The slide rules (minimal text, images, no laser pointer) transfer universally. The board preference often doesn't.
